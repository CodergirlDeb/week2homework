Double Green Chili Burger Recipe

Prep: 20 Minutes Cook: 15 Minutes

Serves: 2

"A spicy, tasty burger loaded with melted american cheese, sweet onions and the star of the show, green chilis"

Ingredients

  • 500g Minced beef (20% fat)
  • 4 Brioche buns
  • 6 American cheese slices
  • 8 Green chillies
  • 1 Red onion
  • Pickles
  • Butter
  • Sale & pepper
  • Pinch of comun & garlic powder
  • Ketchup & mayo

Method

  1. Roast the green chillies under a hot grill untill they've charred al over. Once blackened, leave them in a sealed container to steam for 5 mins. Then, rub the outer layer to remove the blackened parts and chop into slices
  2. Fry the chopped green chillies with some butter and season with a pinch of cumin & garlic powder, the cumin adds a nice smoky flavour but you don't want too much. Once done, set aside.
  3. Chop the red onion how you prefer, I diced but I reccomend strips as they'll shrink. Fry them in some butter, stir untill they are caramelised. Use a good sized pan as you'll be using the same pan for your burgers to soak up the extra flavour from the onions. Once done, set aside.
  4. Butter the buns and toast them in a pan untill nice and golden. Once golden, dress your bottom bun with ketchup, mayo, pickles, red onions & green chillies.
  5. Make 75-80g balls out of mince, don't pack them too tightly as the space between them soaks up all the flavour!
  6. In the pan used to caramelise the onions, add butter and wait for the pan to get hot. Add the meatballs and use something heave (I used the bottom of a pan) to smash down your patties for 10-15 seconds. Season generously with salt & pepper and flip.
  7. Add an american cheese slice on each patty, allow to melt for a few seconds (cover with a lid to speed this up) and put the patties on top of eachother./li>
  8. Place the delicious cheese double patties on the dressed buns, you can add more toppings (I did as I had extra) then place on the top bun.
  9. ENJOY!
